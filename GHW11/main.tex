\section*{Problem 4}
\begin{equation*}
\begin{split}
    1 &= \det(I)\\
    1 &= \det(QQ^T)\\
    1 &= \det(Q)\det(Q^T)\\
    1 &= \det(Q)\det(Q)\\
    1 &= (\det(Q))^2 \implies \\
    \det(Q) &= \pm 1
\end{split}
\end{equation*}
Therefore the determinant of an orthonormal basis $Q$ must be either $1$ or $-1$.
